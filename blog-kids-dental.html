Kids Dental

When Should Your Child
First Visit a Dentist?

The answer might surprise you — and getting it right protects your child's smile for a lifetime.

Kids Dental 📅 20 May 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 👤 Dr. Anjali Desai, MDS Pedodontics

One of the most common questions we get from parents at PureSmile Dental is: "At what age should my child see a dentist for the first time?" Most assume it's when the child starts school, or when they get all their teeth. The correct answer is much earlier — and the reasons why matter enormously for your child's long-term health.

The IDA Recommendation: By Age 1

The Indian Dental Association (IDA) and the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry both recommend scheduling a child's first dental visit within 6 months of their first tooth appearing, or by their first birthday — whichever comes first.

This is not just about checking for cavities. The first visit is primarily about:

  • Assessing proper tooth and jaw development
  • Educating parents on correct oral hygiene for infants
  • Identifying early signs of nursing bottle cavities
  • Building a positive, fear-free relationship with the dentist
  • Checking for tongue-tie or lip-tie issues that can affect feeding and speech
💡 Why Age 1?

Baby teeth are not "just temporary." They hold space for permanent teeth, help with speech development, and are essential for chewing. A cavity in a baby tooth can spread to the permanent tooth developing underneath it.

What Happens at a Child's First Dental Visit?

At PureSmile Dental, we make the first visit a fun, stress-free experience. We call it the "Happy Visit." Here is what to expect:

  1. Meet & Greet: The dentist and team introduce themselves to your child in a friendly, playful way. We let children explore the chair and tools before any examination.
  2. Gentle Examination: We check for tooth eruption patterns, gum health, jaw development, and bite alignment. For very young children, this is done with the child sitting on the parent's lap — called the "knee-to-knee" position.
  3. Parent Education: We show you how to clean your baby's gums before teeth appear, how to brush your toddler's teeth, and what foods to avoid to prevent early childhood cavities.
  4. Risk Assessment: We evaluate your child's diet, feeding habits, and fluoride exposure to assess their cavity risk and create a personalised prevention plan.
  5. No pressure, no stress: If the child is anxious, we stop. The goal of the first visit is trust, not treatment.

The #1 Cause of Dental Problems in Indian Children

In India, nursing bottle syndrome (also called early childhood caries) is extremely common and entirely preventable. It happens when babies and toddlers are put to bed with a bottle of milk, formula, or juice. The sugary liquid pools around the teeth overnight, creating the perfect environment for cavity-causing bacteria.

⚠️ Important for Indian Parents

Many grandparents and caregivers in India dip pacifiers in honey or jaggery to soothe babies. This practice directly causes severe early childhood cavities. Even small amounts of sugar on baby teeth can cause significant damage.

Simple Rules to Prevent Baby Cavities

  • Never put a child to bed with a bottle of milk or juice
  • Switch from bottle to sippy cup by 12–18 months
  • Begin brushing with a soft brush as soon as the first tooth appears
  • Use a rice-grain sized amount of fluoride toothpaste for children under 3
  • Limit sugary snacks and drinks, including "health" drinks like Horlicks or Complan between meals
  • First dental visit by age 1

When Should Kids Start Orthodontic Evaluation?

Many parents wait until all permanent teeth are in before thinking about braces. But early evaluation at age 6–7 can identify problems that are much easier (and cheaper) to treat while the jaw is still growing:

  • Crossbites — where upper and lower teeth don't meet correctly
  • Severe crowding — insufficient jaw space for permanent teeth
  • Protruding front teeth (buck teeth) — injury risk and future alignment issues
  • Early or late loss of baby teeth
  • Mouth breathing habits that affect jaw development

At PureSmile Dental, our orthodontic screening for children is free with any routine checkup.

How Often Should Children Visit the Dentist?

Once your child has had their first visit, we recommend a check-up every 6 months. This allows us to:

  • Catch cavities when they are small and easy to treat
  • Apply preventive fluoride treatments
  • Apply dental sealants on permanent molars (a thin protective coating that prevents up to 80% of cavities)
  • Monitor jaw and bite development
  • Keep your child comfortable and familiar with the dental environment
💡 Make Dentistry Positive

Children who have positive early dental experiences are far less likely to develop dental anxiety as adults. Never use the dentist as a threat ("If you don't brush, the dentist will pull your teeth!"). This creates fear that can prevent people from seeking care when they need it most.

Frequently Asked Questions

My child has no complaints — do they still need a visit?

Absolutely. Most cavities in children cause no pain until they are quite advanced. Regular check-ups catch problems early, when they are simplest and cheapest to treat.

My child is terrified of dentists. What should I do?

Bring them to PureSmile for a "Happy Visit" — no treatment, just a look around, meet the team, and a small reward. We specialise in anxious children and have techniques like tell-show-do, distraction, and nitrous oxide sedation for those who need extra help.

Are dental X-rays safe for children?

Yes. Digital X-rays at PureSmile emit 80–90% less radiation than traditional X-rays. The risk of undetected cavities between teeth far outweighs the minimal radiation exposure.

At PureSmile Dental, a child's routine check-up starts at ₹350. Cleaning and fluoride treatment packages are available from ₹600. Dental sealants per tooth start at ₹800.
