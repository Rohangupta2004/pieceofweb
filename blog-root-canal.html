5 Signs You Need a Root Canal
(And What To Expect)

Modern root canal treatment is painless, quick, and saves your natural tooth. Here is how to know if you need one.

Oral Hygiene 📅 15 June 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 👤 Dr. Priya Sharma, MDS

Root canal treatment is one of the most misunderstood dental procedures in India. Many patients delay it out of fear — only to end up with a tooth extraction that could have been avoided. The truth? A modern root canal at PureSmile Dental is no more uncomfortable than getting a filling. Let's look at the warning signs and bust the myths.

What Is a Root Canal?

Inside every tooth is a soft tissue called the pulp — it contains nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue. When bacteria infect this pulp (due to deep decay, a cracked tooth, or injury), the tissue becomes inflamed and dies. A root canal removes this infected pulp, cleans the inside of the tooth, and seals it to prevent re-infection. Your natural tooth stays intact.

💡 Key Insight

Root canal treatment relieves pain — it does not cause it. The pain you feel before a root canal is from the infection, not the treatment itself.

5 Warning Signs You May Need a Root Canal

1. Persistent, Throbbing Toothache

If you have a deep, aching pain in a tooth that does not go away on its own — especially when you chew or apply pressure — this is one of the strongest indicators of pulp infection. Unlike a regular toothache, this pain is often constant and may radiate to your jaw, ear, or neck.

2. Prolonged Sensitivity to Hot or Cold

A brief sharp pain when you eat ice cream is normal. But if sensitivity lingers for 30 seconds or more after the stimulus is removed, it signals that the nerve inside the tooth may be damaged or dying. This is especially true if cold sensitivity has recently turned into pain from hot foods or drinks.

3. A Pimple or Boil on the Gum

A small, pimple-like bump on your gum near a painful tooth is called a dental abscess or sinus tract. This is your body trying to drain the infection. It may appear and disappear but never truly heals. This is a medical urgency — delaying treatment allows the infection to spread to your jaw, neck, or even your bloodstream.

⚠️ Do Not Ignore This

A dental abscess can become life-threatening if the infection spreads beyond the mouth. If you have severe swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing along with tooth pain, seek emergency dental care immediately.

4. Darkening or Discolouration of the Tooth

When the pulp inside a tooth dies, it can cause the tooth to turn grey, brown, or black from the inside out. This discolouration is not just a cosmetic issue — it is a sign that the tooth's internal tissue has broken down and needs treatment. A single dark tooth in an otherwise healthy mouth almost always points to pulp death.

5. Swollen or Tender Gums Near a Tooth

Swelling, tenderness, or raised gum tissue near a specific tooth — particularly when combined with any of the above symptoms — indicates that infection has spread to the surrounding bone and gum tissue. The swelling may be mild or dramatic. Either way, it needs immediate attention.

What Happens During a Root Canal at PureSmile?

Here is what to expect during your appointment:

  1. Diagnosis & X-ray: We take a digital X-ray to assess the extent of infection and confirm the need for root canal treatment.
  2. Local Anaesthesia: The area around your tooth is thoroughly numbed. You will feel pressure but no pain. We use advanced numbing techniques — most patients are surprised by how comfortable the process is.
  3. Pulp Removal: A tiny opening is made in the crown of the tooth. The infected pulp is carefully removed using precision rotary instruments.
  4. Cleaning & Shaping: The root canals are cleaned, shaped, and disinfected with antimicrobial solution.
  5. Filling & Sealing: The canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed.
  6. Crown Placement: In most cases, a dental crown is placed on top within a few days to protect the tooth long-term.

The entire procedure typically takes 1–2 sittings. After treatment, mild soreness for 2–3 days is normal and managed easily with over-the-counter painkillers.

💰 Save Money by Acting Early

The longer you wait, the more complex (and expensive) the treatment becomes. Catching an infected tooth early often means a simpler, cheaper procedure. A dental abscess left untreated can result in tooth loss requiring an implant — costing 5-10x more.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a root canal painful?

No. With modern anaesthesia and rotary techniques, the procedure is typically pain-free. Most patients report that it feels similar to getting a filling done. The pain you feel before the procedure (from the infection) is far worse than the treatment itself.

How long does recovery take?

Most patients return to normal activities the same day or the next day. Mild soreness for 2–3 days is normal.

Can I eat normally after a root canal?

Avoid hard or chewy foods for 24–48 hours after treatment, and until your crown is placed. After that, you can eat normally.

How long does a root canal last?

With a proper crown placed on top, a root canal treated tooth can last a lifetime with normal oral hygiene habits.
